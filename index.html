What we handle

Three problems. One process.

Negative & Fake Review Removal

Reviews that break Google's policies — fake, off-topic, conflict of interest, or harassment — can be reported and removed. We check eligibility first and only proceed if there's a real path to removal.

  • Google Business Profile reviews
  • Yelp, Trustpilot, and industry directories
  • Policy-violation assessment before you pay

DMCA Takedowns

For original content — photos, copy, design work — that's been copied without permission. We file proper copyright takedown notices directly with hosts and platforms, not mass-reporting shortcuts that get ignored.

  • Stolen website or product content
  • Copied images, video, or written material
  • Direct filing with hosting providers & platforms

Reputation Management

Impersonation, exposed personal information, and harassment campaigns don't fall under copyright — they need a different path. We identify the right policy or legal route for each case.

  • Impersonation & fake profile reports
  • Personal information exposure
  • Search result suppression guidance

Why ClearRep

We tell you when something can't be removed — before you pay for it.

Most reputation damage isn't actually fixable through every channel — a bad review might not qualify under platform policy, or a piece of content might not legally qualify as copyright infringement. We've found that being upfront about what will and won't work builds more trust than promising results we can't back. So we check first, explain the real path, and only move forward when there's a legitimate route to resolution.
